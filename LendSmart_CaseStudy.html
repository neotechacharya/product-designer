Redesigning the Autopay Payment Experience

How we eliminated a surprise decision modal, surfaced Autopay context from the very first screen, and gave users confidence and control over their payments.

How this resolves a complex UX problem: Handling manual payments alongside an active autopay schedule is notoriously complex for users. This redesign solves this friction by:

• Mental Model Alignment: Forcing intent selection first perfectly aligns the system with the user's natural thought process, preventing errors before they occur.
• Progressive Disclosure: Intricate system rules (like locked calendar dates or interest vs. principal splits) are only exposed when contextually relevant, drastically reducing cognitive load.
• Radical Transparency: The dynamic "What Happens to Your Autopay" block translates complex backend logic into plain English, instantly eliminating the anxiety of double-charging.
Fix #3 — The Modal Problem

The Late-Stage Modal Is Completely Eliminated

The old modal forced a binary "Override or Extra?" choice after 3 full steps — with no explanation. In the redesign, this decision is integrated into Step 1 where it belongs.

BeforeAfter
✕ Original: Surprise Modal After Step 3
Problems:
• Appears AFTER user completed 3 full steps — surprise ambush.
• "Override" — cancel permanently? Just once? No explanation.
• "Extra payment" — extra on top of what? When? No specifics.
↓
✓ Redesigned: Decision Integrated Into Step 1
🎉
No More Surprise Modals

The intent decision (Extra / Override / Independent) is now the first thing users see in Step 1 — not a popup after Step 3.

✓ Extra Payment ✓ Override ✓ Independent ✓ Full Payoff
What changed:
• Intent is a first-class selection at the START of the flow.
• Each option has a plain-language description of consequences.
• Zero modals in the entire flow.

Show the Full Picture Before and After Payment

The original confirmation page asked for authorization without disclosing the Autopay impact, and the success screen offered no reassurance. Both screens are now fully transparent, eliminating pre-payment hesitation and post-payment anxiety.

Give Users Clarity About Current Loan Status and Details

Foundation for Trust: By making fixed terms transparent upfront, users feel more confident when deciding how their manual payments will impact the overall timeline.
