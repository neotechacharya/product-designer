CallHippo AI Copilot

How I designed an AI-powered coaching layer that turns every sales call into a learning opportunity

Problem

Sales and support teams were flying blind on every call

CallHippo serves thousands of sales and support teams who make millions of calls every month. Yet despite having call recording in place, managers had no scalable way to evaluate what was actually happening in those conversations.

Coaches were manually listening to random call samples — a process that was slow, biased, and caught only a fraction of issues. Agents received feedback days after a call happened, making it nearly impossible to connect coaching to actual behavior. New reps took weeks to ramp up because feedback loops were too slow and too vague.

The result: poor calls kept happening, deals kept slipping, and customer satisfaction remained inconsistent — not because agents lacked effort, but because they lacked visibility.

Why This Problem? — Auditing the Current Process

Before jumping to solutions, I spent two weeks deeply understanding how managers and agents were currently operating. I interviewed 14 people across sales, support, and operations teams.

Two distinct roles — each with a different relationship to call quality

SM

Sales Manager

“I want to know which reps are struggling and why — but I can't listen to 200 calls a week. I just go off gut feeling and lagging quota numbers.”

TL

Support Team Lead

“I review maybe 5–10 calls per agent per month. By the time I give feedback, they've already made the same mistake 50 more times.”

The existing tools were failing all three roles

No Real-Time Signal

Managers received performance data only through manual reviews or end-of-month quota reports. There was no signal during or immediately after calls.

Subjective Scoring

Call quality evaluation was done manually, meaning two managers would score the same call differently. There was no standardized framework.

Fragmented Tooling

Call recordings lived in one place, CRM notes in another, and coaching feedback in email threads or spreadsheets. Nothing was connected.

Delayed Feedback Loop

The average time between a call happening and an agent receiving feedback was 4–6 days. By then, context was lost and behavior had already solidified.

Research — Understanding Each Role's Real Pain

I conducted remote observation sessions and 1:1 interviews to go deeper than surface complaints and identify the root causes.

Insight 01

Managers don't have a coaching problem. They have a data problem.

They weren't bad coaches. They simply had no consistent, scalable data to coach from. When asked "how do you decide who to coach?", most answered: "whoever had a bad week on quota" or "whoever a customer complained about."

Insight 02

Agents want feedback, but fear ambiguity.

Reps genuinely wanted to improve, but vague feedback like "be more confident" or "listen more" with no data behind it felt unfair and unmotivating. They wanted specifics.

Insight 03

The talk-to-listen ratio was a hidden signal nobody was tracking.

In interviews, every experienced manager mentioned instinctively — "good reps talk less and listen more." Yet this was never measured. It was pure intuition, unscalable across a team.

Insight 04

Sentiment shifts during calls were completely invisible.

A call could start positive and end frustrated — or vice versa — with no one tracking where things shifted and why.

Core Challenges Summarized

Managers

For Managers: No visibility without manual effort.

Their decision-making was reactive, not proactive. They could not see trends across their team without spending hours in call recordings.

Team Leads

For Team Leads: Feedback was slow, subjective, and disconnected from behavior change.

Without objective data, coaching conversations felt personal rather than data-driven — creating friction and defensiveness.

Brainstorming & Scoping — Choosing the Right MVP

After synthesizing research, I ran a prioritization workshop with the PM and engineering lead. We mapped potential features against user impact and build complexity.

We asked: How Might We...

  • Help managers evaluate call quality without listening to every recording?
  • Give agents immediate, objective feedback after each call?
  • Surface patterns across hundreds of calls automatically?
  • Make coaching conversations data-driven instead of opinion-driven?

Four capabilities emerged as the MVP core

Sentiment Analysis

Real-time emotional tone tracking per call, surfacing frustration, satisfaction, and neutrality across the conversation arc.

Topic Extraction

Automatic identification of key themes discussed on each call without manual tagging.

Talk-to-Listen Ratio

Measurable split of speaking time between agent and customer, compared against team benchmarks.

Call Performance Rating

A composite score per call based on duration, sentiment outcome, talk ratio, and CSAT signals — replacing subjective manual scoring.

Features like real-time agent prompting and AI-generated email follow-ups were scoped to Phase 2.

Design — Feature by Feature

Feature 01

Sentiment Analysis

The Problem it solves

Managers had no way to know whether a call ended on a positive or negative note without listening to the whole recording.

Design Decisions

The Sentiment Analytics view surfaces three emotional states — Negative, Neutral, and Positive — each represented as a progress bar with a percentage score. The visual hierarchy is intentional: emoji-based icons make the three states instantly recognizable without reading labels. The bar lengths give managers a proportional sense of distribution at a glance — in this example, 65% neutral, 30% positive, and only 5% negative — so they can immediately judge team health and prioritize where coaching attention is needed.

Impact

The goal was to eliminate random call sampling entirely. Instead of managers guessing which calls to review, the Negative Sentiments flag surfaces exactly which calls need attention — without listening to a single recording.

Feature 02

Topic Extraction

The Problem it solves

Sales and support leaders had no way to know what customers were actually talking about across hundreds of calls. Themes like pricing concerns, coverage questions, or specific product topics were buried in unstructured recordings — completely invisible without someone manually listening through them.

Design Decisions

Key Topics are auto-extracted from each call and displayed as chip tags — for example: medicare charges, $0 premium dental, hearing coverage, medication costs. No manual tagging, no configuration needed. The chips surface automatically, giving managers an instant read of what dominated each conversation the moment a call ends. The compact chip format was intentional — scannable in under 3 seconds, without opening a single recording.

Impact

Managers stopped spending time guessing what customers cared about. The auto-extracted topics gave teams a real, unfiltered view of recurring customer language at scale — directly informing coaching priorities and product feedback in a way that manual review never could.

Feature 03

Talk-to-Listen Ratio

The Problem it solves

"Listen more" was the most common coaching advice managers gave — but it was completely unactionable. There was no measurement, no benchmark, and no way to track whether anything actually changed after feedback was given.

Design Decisions

The UI breaks conversation time into three distinct components — Customer Speak Time, Agent Speak Time, and Dead Air — each shown as a separate bar with a precise percentage. This was a deliberate decision over a simple two-way split. Dead Air is the hidden insight: 40.40% silence in a call is often where deals are lost or customers disengage, yet it was never tracked before. Separating it from speaking time forces managers to ask a question they were never asking — what's happening in the silence?

Color coding reinforces meaning immediately — green for customer voice, orange for agent voice, red for dead air — so the health of a call is readable in under 3 seconds without interpreting numbers.

Impact

Team leads began using talk ratio as the standard opening point in 1:1 coaching sessions — replacing gut-feel with a shared, objective reference.

Feature 04

Call Summary

The Problem it solves

After every call, agents had no record of what was discussed, what was promised, or what the next step was. Key details lived only in memory — leading to missed follow-ups, repeated questions, and inconsistent CRM notes.

Design Decisions

The Call Summary auto-generates a structured recap immediately after a call ends — covering key topics discussed, customer intent, agent actions, and suggested next steps. The goal was zero manual effort from the agent. Rather than asking agents to write notes while their next call is already queued, the summary is ready before they've hung up. The language is kept conversational, not technical, so agents can forward it directly to a CRM or a customer without editing.

Impact

The goal was to eliminate the gap between what happened on a call and what got recorded. A consistent, auto-generated summary means CRM data stays accurate, follow-ups don't fall through, and managers can review call context without listening to the recording.

Results

CallHippo AI Copilot was designed for 200+ sales and support teams making calls at scale daily.

The core shift the design aimed to create was moving managers from reactive coaching — triggered only by quota drops or customer complaints — to proactive development anchored in consistent, automatic call data.

For agents, the goal was equally significant: replacing the anxiety of infrequent, opaque review cycles with continuous, self-readable performance signals. When agents can see their own talk ratio, sentiment pattern, and call summary immediately after a call — improvement becomes self-directed rather than manager-dependent.

One outcome that emerged beyond the original scope: Topic Extraction became a product research tool. Teams began routing keyword data to product and marketing — a use case never planned, but one that reflects how well-designed ambient data can create value far beyond its original intent.

What I Learned

  1. 1. Transparency in AI outputs is not optional.

    Every time we showed how a score was calculated, trust and adoption improved. Black-box outputs feel arbitrary; explainable outputs feel fair.

  2. 2. The emotional framing of data matters as much as the data itself.

    The same number presented as a "grade" creates defensiveness. Presented as a "coaching index with growth trend," it creates ownership.

  3. 3. Build for the skeptic first.

    The hardest users to win over became the strongest advocates once the design addressed their objections.

  4. 4. Secondary use cases are often the most valuable.

    Topic Extraction became a strategic asset for product and marketing teams via authentic customer language at scale.
